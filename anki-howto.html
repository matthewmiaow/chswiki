Anki tutorial

From the academic commitee

So spoke Gandalf the wise.

Most of the flashcards on this site come as an .apkg format. What can you do with that? Well, you can load these files into a program called Anki, which will schedule flashcard revisions for you, so you don't need to cram (ideally.)

How to load a file into Anki

Step 1: Click on the link to the Flashcards from the main Wiki page.
Step 2: You should get a pop-up asking you to save the .apkg file. Do that.
Step 3: Download Anki for your computer here.
Step 4: Open up Anki.
Step 5: Click the "File" tab in the top menu, and navigate to "Import". Select the .apkg file you downloaded earlier.
Step 6: Once the file is imported, simply click on its name in the main Anki page to start studying.

How to use Anki properly

When you open a flashcard, you should get four options--"Again", "Hard", "Good" and "Easy". Depending on the tab you click, Anki will schedule your next revision of the card to be further away or closer in the future. For cards you are just learning, you can click "Again", and Anki will prompt you for that flashcard soon. For cards you already know, you can clicl "Good" or "Easy", and Anki will prompt you for the card--depending on how well you know it--:w in the next few days, weeks, or months.
